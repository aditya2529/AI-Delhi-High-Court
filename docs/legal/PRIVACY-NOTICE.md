# Privacy Notice

**Effective date:** 2026-05-17
**Service:** Delhi HC Case Tracker (MVP)

This page tells you, in plain language, what we collect when you use this site, why, how long we keep it, and how to ask us to delete it. We are not the Delhi High Court. This site is an independent tool that helps you query the Court's publicly available case-status search.

## Who we are

A small, independent project. Contact for any privacy question, correction, or deletion request: **\<privacy-contact-email\>**. We aim to respond within five working days.

## What we collect

When you run a search, we receive and process:

- **The case search you typed** — case type, case number, year. You typed this yourself; we do not infer it. We treat it as the minimum necessary to perform the service you asked for.
- **A one-way hash of your IP address.** We never store your raw IP address. The hash lets us detect abuse (for example, one source flooding the service) without keeping data that points back to you personally.
- **A timestamp** of when the search happened.
- **The technical result** of the call to the Delhi High Court website (success, failure, timing). This is needed to operate the service and to be polite to the Court's servers.

We do **not** collect:

- Your name, email address, phone number, or any account credentials. The site has no login.
- Your location beyond what an IP hash implies (which is nothing on its own).
- Cookies for tracking, analytics, or advertising. The only cookie we set is a session cookie that holds your in-progress CAPTCHA session and is deleted when you close the tab.
- Your device fingerprint, browser history, or anything from other sites.

We never store the contents of court cases beyond the seconds needed to show you the result. We do not build a database of cases, parties, or judgments.

## Why we collect it

- **The search query** is what you asked us to run. Without it, the service does nothing.
- **The IP hash and timestamp** let us detect and slow down abuse so that we remain a polite client to the Court's website and keep the service available for everyone.

We rely on the "legitimate use" basis under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, specifically performing the service you yourself initiated.

## How long we keep it

- **Search request records:** retained for **90 days**, then anonymised by stripping the case query and keeping only counts. The anonymised aggregate may be kept indefinitely for capacity planning.
- **Outbound request log** (our calls to the Court's website, for operational debugging): retained for **30 days**, then deleted.
- **Session data** (your in-progress CAPTCHA session): deleted when you close the tab or after 15 minutes of inactivity, whichever comes first.

We do not back these up to long-term archives.

## Who we share it with

Nobody. We do not sell, rent, share, or transfer your data to advertisers, analytics providers, data brokers, or any third party. We will share data only if compelled by a valid Indian legal order, and we will tell you on this page if that ever happens.

The Delhi High Court's own website, of course, receives the search request you submit — it has to, to answer the question — but that is your direct interaction with the Court, not data we forward beyond what is needed to perform your search.

## Your rights

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, you may ask us to:

- confirm what data we hold that relates to you,
- correct or update it,
- erase it,
- nominate someone to act on your behalf, or
- raise a grievance.

Email **\<privacy-contact-email\>** with enough detail for us to find the record (for example, the approximate date and the search you ran). Because we hold a hashed IP and not your identity, we may need you to demonstrate the request relates to you (for example, by sending from the same network). We will not ask for more identifying information than is needed to act on your request.

## Children

This service is not designed for or directed at children under 18. We do not knowingly collect data from minors.

## Changes

If we change this notice, we will update the effective date at the top and post a notice on the site for at least 30 days.

## Final note

The Delhi High Court's own website is the authoritative source for case information. If our display and theirs disagree, theirs is correct.
